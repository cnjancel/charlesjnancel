Overview

Welcome to the College Football Performance Analysis project. This project provides a comprehensive analysis of college football games over the past twenty years, exploring various factors influencing game dynamics and team performance.

Executive Summary

This report delves deeply into a dataset that covers twenty years of college football games, marked by significant changes and advancements in the sport. Our analysis utilizes this data to explore factors influencing team performance and the excitement level of the games.

Our findings emphasize the importance of ELO ratings and the excitement index in predicting game outcomes and audience engagement, providing invaluable insights for stakeholders in the college football industry.

Data Loading and Preparation

The dataset was meticulously cleaned and prepared to ensure accuracy. Key steps included removing columns with high missingness, imputing missing values for critical variables such as attendance and excitement index, and filtering out rows with missing ELO ratings.

Conclusion

Despite the comprehensive nature of the dataset, potential exclusions of factors like weather conditions or player injuries can impact game outcomes. Future analyses could explore these additional variables or employ advanced statistical methods to enhance understanding.

Stakeholders should be cautious in applying these insights, considering potential biases and dataset limitations.
