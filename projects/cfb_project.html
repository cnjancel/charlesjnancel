Overview

This project delves into two decades of college football games, exploring factors influencing team performance and the excitement level of games. Key data points include team scores, game dates, and an 'excitement index,' which helps in understanding the dynamics of audience engagement.

Results

The analysis highlights key factors such as home and away ELO ratings, showing strong connections with game outcomes. The 'excitement index' was found to significantly correlate with audience engagement, suggesting more thrilling games attract larger audiences.

Discussion

Our findings offer valuable insights for teams and stakeholders. Recognizing the influence of ELO ratings can guide performance strategies, while understanding the excitement index's impact helps optimize audience engagement tactics.
